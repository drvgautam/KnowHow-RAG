Answer Confidence Tiers

Communicating certainty to users — high, medium, and low confidence

Why tiers matter

Users treat all AI-generated answers equally unless you tell them otherwise. A confidence tier system gives users the context they need to decide how much to trust each answer and whether to verify it themselves.

The three tiers

  • High confidence — Retrieval score above threshold, high faithfulness score, single authoritative source.
    UI treatment: normal answer display with green indicator. User can trust this answer for most purposes.
  • Medium confidence — Retrieval score is moderate, or multiple sources partially agree, or faithfulness score has minor issues.
    UI treatment: answer displayed with amber indicator and a note: "This answer may not be complete. Please verify with the source documents."
  • Low confidence — Retrieval score is near the gate threshold, or sources conflict, or faithfulness score is low.
    UI treatment: answer displayed with red indicator, prominent warning, and direct links to source documents for manual review.

Calculating the tier

The tier is a composite of three signals:

  • Retrieval confidence: The cross-encoder score of the top retrieved chunk
  • Source agreement: Do multiple retrieved chunks agree or contradict?
  • Faithfulness score: The NLI model's assessment of the generated answer

A simple weighted formula or a decision tree maps these three signals to one of the three tiers.

User experience impact

Studies show that users who see confidence tiers:

  • Are 3x more likely to verify low-confidence answers
  • Report higher trust in the system overall (because they feel it's being honest)
  • Are less likely to propagate incorrect information

The counterintuitive finding: showing users that the system is sometimes uncertain actually increases their overall trust, not decreases it. Overconfident systems that are occasionally wrong erode trust much faster.
